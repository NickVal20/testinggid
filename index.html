The 7 wonders of the world

What are the 7 wonders of the world

In ancient times, civilizations built monumental structures symbolizing their peak achievements in architecture and art, each with a unique purpose and story. These wonders included a towering tomb and a massive statue at a harbor, both honoring rulers and gods. An immense lighthouse guided sailors along Egypt’s coast, while a grand temple dedicated to a goddess showcased cultural pride. A Persian queen built an intricately decorated tomb for her husband, and a king created lush gardens for his queen in a desert city. Finally, Egypt’s great pyramid—built with stunning precision—still stands, representing ancient ingenuity and legacy.

Our Popular Courses

Great Wall of China (China): A series of walls built across northern China over centuries to protect against invasions, stretching over 13,000 miles.
Petra (Jordan): An ancient city carved into red sandstone cliffs by the Nabataeans, known for its intricate architecture, especially the Treasury and Monastery.
Christ the Redeemer (Brazil): A 30-meter-tall statue of Jesus Christ atop Mount Corcovado in Rio de Janeiro, symbolizing peace and hospitality.
Machu Picchu (Peru): A 15th-century Inca citadel located in the Andes Mountains, renowned for its well-preserved ruins and advanced engineering.
Chichen Itza (Mexico): A large Mayan archaeological site famous for its step-pyramid, El Castillo, and advanced knowledge of astronomy and mathematics.
Roman Colosseum (Italy): An ancient amphitheater in Rome, built around 80 CE, that hosted gladiatorial contests, public spectacles, and more.
Taj Mahal (India): A white marble mausoleum built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, symbolizing eternal love.
